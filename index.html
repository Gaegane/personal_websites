Education

2013 - 2015

primary

My primary school years at Dikakanyo were full of learning and self-discovery. While academics were important and I managed to do well, particularly in Natural Sciences, what really shaped me were the extracurricular activities. Gumboot dancing became my favorite pastime, and our team’s success in competitions gave me a sense of achievement and pride. Those experiences built my confidence and taught me the value of teamwork, lessons I carry with me to this day. I was always respectful and focused on my studies, and primary school played a key role in shaping the person I’ve become.

2013 - 2015

secondary

After completing primary school, I enrolled at Kgosi Kebalipile Secondary School. Unfortunately, my experience there was far from positive. The school had serious issues, including corruption that extended to the principal. It was during this time that I began to see the clear differences between public and private education. Many of the students were caught up in negative influences like drugs, and skipping classes was common. The lack of support from teachers made it even harder to stay focused. Failing Grade 10 was a difficult and painful moment for me, as it was my first time repeating a year. I felt discouraged and thought about giving up on school entirely. However, my grandmother’s encouragement helped me push through, and I decided to transfer schools, moving to Johannesburg in search of a better environment and new opportunities.
